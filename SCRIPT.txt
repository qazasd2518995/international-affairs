================================================================================
                 MINI DEBATE ARENA — FULL MC SCRIPT
                         (English, 4 speakers)
================================================================================

CAST
  JUSTIN    — Platform / Tech guide. Operates the app from the laptop.
  BENJAMIN  — Main MC / Host. Runs the flow, warms up the room, leads debate.
  TIFFANY   — Judge 1 (Logic Lens). Scores + delivers short feedback per match.
  VINCENT   — Judge 2 (Delivery Lens). Scores + delivers short feedback per match.

TOTAL RUNTIME
  ~15-18 minutes end to end.

NOTES
  • [DO:] = an on-stage / on-screen action
  • [NOTE:] = an invisible reminder for the speaker
  • Lines in italics (marked *...*) are optional / improv hooks
  • Prize at the end: NT$500 7-ELEVEN voucher for the champion team


================================================================================
  PART 0 — PRE-SHOW SETUP  (BEFORE CLASS STARTS)
================================================================================

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Laptop connected to projector. Open Chrome. Go to
   https://international-affairs.vercel.app/admin. Click "START NEW GAME".]
  [DO: The SCAN TO JOIN screen + QR code is now projected.]
  [DO: Open /judge on Tiffany's phone → she taps JUDGE 2.]
  [DO: Open /judge on Vincent's phone → he taps JUDGE 1.]
  [DO: Sound check on background music (♪ ON in bottom-right).]


================================================================================
  PART 1 — OPENING & LOBBY  (≈ 2 minutes)
================================================================================

BENJAMIN:
  "Alright everyone, welcome! Today we are doing something a little
   different. Instead of a normal class activity, you are about to play
   a live game — it's called MINI DEBATE ARENA.

   Think of it like a mix between Pokemon battles, game shows, and a
   real debate club. Six teams. Three matches. One champion.

   And yes — the winning team walks away with a 500-dollar 7-ELEVEN
   voucher. *(pause for reaction)* So let's make this count."

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Point at projector]
  "Okay, here's how you join. Everyone take out your phones right now.
   You'll see a big QR code on the screen. Scan it with your camera.

   Once it opens, type your name, and pick your group — you should
   already know your group number. We've set up 6 slots on screen:
   Groups 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7. Group 2 is our team running the show, so
   we're not competing. Tap your group, then hit START BATTLE.

   If you run into any problem, raise your hand. If you close the tab
   by accident later, don't worry — there's always a small QR code in
   the bottom-left of the screen you can re-scan."

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: While students are joining]
  "While you're all scanning in, let me set the vibe. In this game,
   there's no 'right' answer. Every topic has two sides, and whichever
   side you end up defending is decided by the computer — randomly —
   so no one gets to cherry-pick. You might AGREE with something you
   personally disagree with. That's the challenge. That's the fun.

   *(glance at Tiffany and Vincent)* And we have two judges today —
   Tiffany and Vincent — who will score every match. Plus, the rest
   of you in the audience also get to vote. Your votes count for 30%
   of the final score. So even if you're not on stage, you still
   decide who wins."

JUSTIN:
  [NOTE: Wait until roughly all 29-30 students have joined. Watch the
   "X HEROES JOINED" counter on the projector.]
  "Looks like we've got… [say the number shown] players in. Great.
   Once everyone's ready, I'll click START GAME."

BENJAMIN:
  "Everybody in? Ready? Let's GO!"

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click START GAME.]


================================================================================
  PART 2 — AUTO-PAIRING REVEAL  (≈ 30 seconds)
================================================================================

JUSTIN:
  [DO: The system instantly shuffles 6 groups into 3 pairs and flips
   coins for sides. A big MATCHUPS screen appears.]
  "So the system has just done two things — it randomly paired the
   groups into 3 matchups, and then it flipped a coin to decide who
   argues AGREE and who argues DISAGREE. Here's how it shook out."

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Point at screen as each pair is named]
  "Alright! Match 1 — Group [X] versus Group [Y]. Match 2 —
   [Z] versus [W]. Match 3 — [P] versus [Q].

   Before we start Match 1… let's look at the topic."


================================================================================
  PART 3 — MATCH 1  (≈ 4 minutes)
================================================================================

--- 3A. Topic Draw ---

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click the DRAW A TOPIC card.]
  "Okay, drawing Match 1's topic now. Watch the screen…"

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Narrate the countdown + shuffle animation with energy]
  "Three… two… one… SHUFFLE! *(follow the progress bar)* It's
   slowing down… locking in…"

  [DO: Once revealed, read the topic out loud, slowly and clearly]
  "Our first topic is…  *(pause, read)*

   [TOPIC SHOWN ON SCREEN]

   And look at this — it's a [1-star / 2-star / 3-star] level, in the
   [CATEGORY] category."

  [NOTE: If it's a 3-star ★★★ BOSS LEVEL, add:]
  *"Oooh, BOSS LEVEL — this one's going to be a real test."*

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click REVEAL MATCHUP.]


--- 3B. Matchup Spotlight ---

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Read the pairing dramatically]
  "So for this first round we have…

   Group [X] — you're arguing AGREE. Your job is to convince us this
   statement is TRUE.

   Group [Y] — you're arguing DISAGREE. Your job is to tear it apart.

   Whether you personally believe it or not doesn't matter — pretend
   you do. That's the game."

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click START PREP.]


--- 3C. Preparation  (90 seconds) ---

BENJAMIN:
  "90 seconds on the clock. Prep teams — open your phones right now.
   You will see an INPUT BOX where you type arguments. Here's the
   trick: EVERY teammate on your side can type. And every line you
   submit stays on the record — the AI judge sees all of them.

   So don't all type the same thing. SPLIT UP. One person handles
   evidence, another does examples, another does the 'why does this
   matter' angle. Punch out as many solid points as you can.

   The other four groups — you'll see the live feed on your phones
   too. You're watching both sides build their case. Start forming
   your own opinion about who's going to win, because you'll vote
   later."

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Watch the admin screen. Both teams' attack counters should be
   climbing in real time.]
  [NOTE: If one team hasn't submitted anything with 20s left, whisper
   to Benjamin to prompt them.]

BENJAMIN:
  [NOTE: At the 10-second mark, call out time]
  "Ten seconds left — get those last ideas in!"

  [NOTE: When timer hits 0 OR when both teams have 2-3 attacks each,
   Justin will click BATTLE START.]


--- 3D. Free Debate  (120 seconds) ---

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Wave both teams up to the front of the room]
  "Okay Group [X], Group [Y] — come up here. Stand up, face the class.

   Two minutes. Free debate. Go back and forth — one speaks, the other
   responds, then flip. Use the arguments you just typed. Don't just
   read them, PITCH them.

   On my go… *(Justin clicks BATTLE START)*

   GO!"

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click BATTLE START. Big 2:00 timer appears.]

BENJAMIN:
  [NOTE: During the 2 minutes, Benjamin is the debate moderator. If
   one team dominates, cut in with:]
  *"Let's hear a response from the other side."*
  *"Can Group [X] answer that directly?"*
  *"30 seconds left — best final point wins this round."*

  [NOTE: If debate dies early, throw in a provocation:]
  *"But what about [counterpoint the other side hasn't raised]?"*


--- 3E. Audience Cheer Vote  (20 seconds) ---

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Timer hits 0 automatically, or click End Debate early. Phase
   advances to AUDIENCE CHEER.]
  "Okay, who won the room? The four groups who weren't on stage — open
   your phones, you'll see a voting screen. Tap the team you found
   more convincing. You have 20 seconds."

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: While audience votes, keep it lively]
  "This is your moment, audience. Think about who actually made you
   rethink the issue — not who yelled the loudest. Who had the
   example that stuck? Who stayed on topic? That's who you vote for."


--- 3F. Judges Score ---

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Voting timer ends. Phase auto-advances to SCORING.]
  "Judges are scoring now."

BENJAMIN:
  "While Tiffany and Vincent lock in their scores — quick note to
   everyone: our judges each look at things differently. Tiffany is
   the LOGIC judge — reasoning, evidence, structure. Vincent is the
   DELIVERY judge — clarity, confidence, persuasion. Same debate, two
   lenses. That's why a good team wins both."

TIFFANY:
  [DO: On her phone, she sees 1-10 sliders for both teams + an AI
   suggestion with a logic-focused commentary. She picks her scores
   and taps SUBMIT.]
  [NOTE: Wait for Justin to advance phase before speaking]

VINCENT:
  [DO: Same on his phone, but AI commentary focuses on delivery.]
  [NOTE: Wait for Justin to advance phase before speaking]

JUSTIN:
  [NOTE: Once both judges are ✓ ready — the screen shows two green
   ticks — click REVEAL RESULT.]


--- 3G. Result Reveal ---

JUSTIN:
  [DO: MatchResult animation plays with confetti.]

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Wait for the winner name to appear on screen, then read with
   drama]
  "And the winner of Match 1… is…  [WINNING GROUP NAME]!

   Congratulations! Round of applause!"

TIFFANY:
  [DO: Deliver a 15-20 second feedback as Judge 1, Logic lens]
  "Quick note from me as the logic judge —
   [EXAMPLE feedback — improvise based on what actually happened:]
   *Group [X], your opening argument about [Y] was really tight. I
   liked that you gave a concrete example. Group [Z], your reasoning
   was a bit looser — you made a strong claim but didn't back it up
   with evidence. Both sides, strong effort.*"

VINCENT:
  [DO: Deliver a 15-20 second feedback as Judge 2, Delivery lens]
  "And from the delivery side —
   [EXAMPLE feedback:]
   *Group [X], you sounded confident, your voice was clear, you stayed
   on topic. Group [Z], your energy was higher but you interrupted a
   few times. Remember, persuasion is also about letting the other
   side finish. Nice work, both of you.*"

BENJAMIN:
  "Great first match! Alright, grab some water, breathe — next match
   coming up."

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click NEXT MATCH. The flow loops back to TOPIC DRAW for the
   next pair.]


================================================================================
  PART 4 — MATCH 2  (≈ 4 minutes, same flow)
================================================================================

BENJAMIN:
  "Alright, Match 2 time. Different topic, different teams, same
   rules. Let's go."

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click DRAW A TOPIC.]

[REPEAT the entire Match 1 sequence:
   Topic Draw → Matchup Spotlight → Prep → Debate → Cheer → Score → Result.
   All the same beats, but adjust the "first match" language — say
   things like "round two", "second battle", etc.]

BENJAMIN (Match 2 intro line variant):
  "Round two. Now we know how this works. Let's see who learned from
   Match 1."

TIFFANY & VINCENT:
  [NOTE: Again give 15-20s feedback each after the result reveal.
   Try to build on what you said in Match 1, e.g.]
  TIFFANY: *"Much sharper structure this time — you clearly absorbed
   the feedback from last round."*
  VINCENT: *"I loved when Group [X] said [specific phrase] — that's
   exactly the kind of clear pitch that wins audiences."*


================================================================================
  PART 5 — MATCH 3  (≈ 4 minutes, final match)
================================================================================

BENJAMIN:
  "Last match! Groups [P] and [Q] — you're up. And this is where
   things get spicy, because after this match we tally the XP from
   all three matches and crown our champion. So if you're one of the
   teams who already won, a good showing here could seal it. If
   you're the underdog… this is your comeback moment."

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click DRAW A TOPIC.]

[REPEAT full sequence as before.]

BENJAMIN (during Match 3 debate):
  *"This is your last shot at XP — leave it all out here!"*

TIFFANY & VINCENT:
  [NOTE: Final match feedback — be a little more generous, it's the
   last time they'll hear from you]


================================================================================
  PART 6 — LEADERBOARD + CHAMPION CROWNING  (≈ 2 minutes)
================================================================================

JUSTIN:
  [DO: After Match 3 result, click CROWN CHAMPION.]
  [DO: Leaderboard appears, sorted by total XP across 3 matches.]

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Look at the leaderboard, then build the tension]
  "Okay. All three matches are done. The leaderboard is LIVE on
   screen, and the math has been done — 70% judge score, 30%
   audience cheer, across all three matches.

   Before we crown the champion, let me just say —
   *(look at the room)*
   Everyone who stood up and debated today, you did something hard.
   You took a position, you defended it, you listened, you
   responded. That's not easy, and most adults never practice it.
   So even if your team didn't win, you actually won.

   Now… the fun part."

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Click into the final-awards screen. Big drumroll animation
   with "WHO IS THE CHAMPION?" flashing.]

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Let the animation build. Read the screen when the crown +
   team name appears]
  "And the CHAMPION of Mini Debate Arena is…

   *(read name)*

   GROUP [X] !!!  *(huge applause, hand it off to the winners)*"

  [DO: Hand the 500-dollar 7-ELEVEN voucher to the winning team.]

TIFFANY:
  [DO: Short closing]
  "Congrats Group [X]! Spend the voucher well — maybe share some
   snacks with the rest of the class."

VINCENT:
  "Real talk though — every team today had moments that would win a
   real debate round. This wasn't close because one team was weak.
   It was close because you all showed up."

BENJAMIN:
  [DO: Wrap up]
  "That's Mini Debate Arena. Thank you to our judges Tiffany and
   Vincent, thank you Justin for running the tech, and thank you
   all for playing.

   Remember — good debate isn't about being loud or being right.
   It's about thinking sharper and listening harder. Do that, and
   you'll win every argument you actually care about.

   See you next class!"

JUSTIN:
  [DO: Fade music out. Close the projector.]


================================================================================
  APPENDIX A — QUICK REFERENCE CHEAT SHEETS
================================================================================

JUSTIN'S CLICK ORDER (the tech spine of the whole show)
  1. START NEW GAME         → lobby with QR
  2. START GAME             → matchups auto-reveal
  3. DRAW A TOPIC           → topic shuffle animation
  4. REVEAL MATCHUP         → pair announcement
  5. START PREP             → 90s timer, students type
  6. BATTLE START!          → 120s debate timer
  7. (timer auto-advances)  → audience vote (20s)
  8. (timer auto-advances)  → judges score
  9. REVEAL RESULT          → winner + confetti
 10. NEXT MATCH             → loop back to step 3 (2 more times)
 11. CROWN CHAMPION         → final awards screen

If something lags: the left-bottom JOIN QR pill is always there.
If students drop off: they can re-scan the QR and rejoin mid-game.


BENJAMIN'S ENERGY CHECKPOINTS
  ● Opening      → hype + set prize stakes
  ● Prep phase   → push multiple teammates to type
  ● Debate       → moderate, don't let one team dominate
  ● Result       → drama + genuine applause
  ● Final        → gracious, credit everyone


TIFFANY'S JUDGE-1 FEEDBACK TEMPLATE (Logic lens)
  "Group [X], your [specific argument] was well-structured because
   [reason]. Group [Y], the weak spot was [specific claim] — you
   needed [evidence / example / logical connection]. Overall, [win
   condition]."


VINCENT'S JUDGE-2 FEEDBACK TEMPLATE (Delivery lens)
  "Group [X], your delivery stood out because [specific moment —
   confident opening, great example, good eye contact, clean
   pitch]. Group [Y], you had energy but [specific thing — got
   tangled in long sentences / interrupted / lost the thread].
   Nice work both."


================================================================================
  APPENDIX B — IF THINGS GO WRONG
================================================================================

SCENARIO                            FIX
---------                           ---
A team doesn't submit attacks       Justin clicks FORCE SKIP, confirms
                                    "yes, skip anyway" — AI will score
                                    with whatever was submitted.

Student phone dies mid-game         They re-scan the bottom-left QR on
                                    the projector and log back in.

Wifi hiccup, phones out of sync     Wait 3-5 seconds — the polling
                                    fallback catches up automatically.

Debate runs long                    Justin clicks "End Debate" — jumps
                                    straight to audience vote.

Judges haven't scored when Justin   A confirm dialog asks "only X of 2
tries to REVEAL RESULT              scored, reveal anyway?" — Justin
                                    checks with Tiffany/Vincent first.

Wrong topic drawn / want to redraw  No in-game redraw. Benjamin covers
                                    it: "Okay, this is the topic,
                                    let's roll with it."

Nobody is winning the debate        Benjamin throws in a provocation
                                    ("but what about X?") to spark it
                                    back up.
